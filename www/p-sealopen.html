Gallegos

Key Point
  • Examined the hemostasis rates of the PKS SEAL Open Forceps and the Valley Lab (Covidien) LigaSure MAX.
  • In most cases the SEAL Open Forceps outperformed the LigaSure system with regard to burst pressure.
  • Seal failures were seen only with the LigaSure device. Failure was defined by the inability of a vessel to withstand 360 mmHG for greater than 10 seconds.
  • PKS SEAL demonstrated a statistically significantly higher likelihood of vessel seal success on primary in-vivo application when compared to the LigaSure MAX.
  • "Gyrus SEAL performs clinically equivalent to LigaSure MAX."
  • "...effective primary seal rates and excellent burst pressure strength provided by the Gyrus SEAL may reduce OR time and complications by minimizing inadvertent hemorrhage."
Reference
  • Gallegos R., Nochek P.,Lahti M., Duning M., Biano R. In-Vivo Assessment of the Gyrus SEAL Open Forceps Intra-Abdominal Procedures in a Chronic Canine Model. Presented at AAGL, 2004.

Presthus

Key Point
  • Examined the vessel securing ability of the PKS SEAL Open Forceps on skelotonized vessels up to 7 mm in diameter to withstand sustained pressure of 300 mmHg using bench and animal models.
  • Mean burst pressure, among all vessel diameters: 1052 mmHg.
  • Thermal spread range, among all vessel diameters: 0.4-2.3 mm from closure site edge.
  • The PKS SEAL Open Forceps provides efficient vessel securing with the ability to withstand high intraluminal pressure.
  • Bench model test: Success rate of 95.5% or 64/67 vessels were secured. Diameters of 4,5,6,7,8, and 10 mm.
  • Animal model test: Success rate of 95.7% or 44/46 vessels were secured. Diameters of 2.5, 3, 3.5, 4,5,6, and 7 mm.
  • End point burst pressures were tested in the animal model and ranged from 565 to 1934 mmHg, with a mean of 1052 mmHg.
  • "Using the open forceps to coagulate tissue rather than conventional clamps decreases the need for instrument exchanges and may ultimately decrease operating time with cost savings."
Reference
  • Presthus JB, Brooks PG, Kirchhof N. Vessel Sealing Using A Pulsed Bipolar System And Open Forceps. J. Am Assoc Gynecol Laparosc. 2003 Nov;10(4): 528-33.
