Dodde

Key Point
  • Examined the tissue temperature at 1 mm from the edge of the Gyrus PKS Cutting Forceps and Ethicon Harmonic ACE (measured in temperature using a thermistor 1 mm from the edge of the device).
  • PKS Cutting Forceps:
    - Average maximum temperature at 1 mm from device edge: 56.8ºC
    - Average time to achieve hemostasis: 5.7 sec.
  • Harmonic Scalpel:
    - Average maximum temperature at 1 mm from device edge:
    - Coagulation setting: 57.5ºC
    - Coagulation until cut setting: 61.9ºC
    - Coagulation and cut setting: 56.4ºC
    • - Average time to achieve hemostasis:
    - Coagulation setting: 5.2 sec.
    - Coagulation until cut setting: 10.3 sec.
    - Coagulation and cut setting: 6.0 sec.
  • No statistically significant difference between the PKS Cutting Forceps or Ethicon Harmonic ACE in thermal temperatures at 1 mm from device edge.
  • The PKS Cutting Forceps and the Ethicon Harmonic ACE demonstrate "comparable thermal profiles during energized dissection and hemostasis."
Reference
  • Dodde R., Shih A., Advincula A. A Novel Technique for Demonstrating the Real-Time Subsurface Tissue Thermal Profile of Two Energized Surgical Instruments. J. Minimally Invasive Gynecology. 2009; 16 (5); p. 599-603.

Erian

Key Point
  • Prospective study examined 400 patients that underwent laparoscopic supracervical hysterectomy (LSH).
  • Average OR time: 46.4 min. *OR time is calculated as the time at which CO2 was started to the closure of the laparoscopic ports.
  • Average uterine weight: 138 grams.
  • Average estimated blood loss: 126 mL.
  • Authors believe that the use of the PKS 45 cm Cutting Forceps contribute to reduced blood loss. The Lap Loop was used for amputation of the uterus.
  • The versatile PK system allows for reduction in instrument change, helping to reduce OR time.
  • For more information regarding LSH, please reference: Bojahr et al. (2009); Cipullo et al. (2009); Einarsson et al. (2010); Ghomi et al. (2007); Hoffman et al. (2005); Sarmini et al. (2005); and Zupi et al. (2003).
Reference
  • Erian J., Hassan M., Pachydakis A., Chandakas S., Wissa L., Hill N. Efficacy Of Laparoscopic Subtotal Hysterectomy In The Management Of Menorrhorhagia: 400 Consecutive Cases. Obstetrics & Gynecology. 2008; 115 (6); p. 742-8.

Ghomi

Key Point
  • Prospective study that examined the learning curve for laparoscopic supracervical hysterectomy (LSH).
  • 60 patients underwent LSH procedure.
  • The OR time was significantly reduced from 166 min. to 142.3 min. between the first 30 and last 30 cases.
  • Average uterine weight: First 30 cases-132.8 grams; last 30 cases-154.6 grams.
  • Average length of hospital stay: First 30 cases-23.6 hours; last 30 cases-23.3 hours.
  • Upper uterine pedicles are coagulated and transected by using a 5 mm PKS device (device not specified).
  • Harmonic Scalpel (Ethicon) used for amputation and Gynecare morcellator used for specimen removal (device not specified).
  • The authors concluded that 30 cases are needed for proficiency in laparoscopic supracervical hysterectomy and that OR time is reduced with experience.
  • For more information regarding LSH, please reference: Erian et al. (2008); Bojahr et al. (2009); Cipullo et al. (2009); Einarsson et al. (2010); Hoffman et al. (2005); Sarmini et al. (2005); and Zupi et al. (2003).
Reference
  • Ghomi A., Littman P., Prasad A., Einarsson J. Assessing The Learning Curve for Laparoscopic Supracervical Hysterectomy. J. of the Society of Laparoendoscopic Surgeons. 2007; 11; p. 190-194.

Hoffman

Key Point
  • Retrospective study, examined the clinical outcomes of abdominal hysterectomy (TAH), laparoscopic supracervical hysterectomy (LSH) and total laparoscopic hysterectomy (TLH).
  • TAH-255; LSH-251; TLH-108 patients.
  • Median OR time, statistically significant: TAH-88 min.; TLH-131 min.; LSH-123 min.
  • Median estimated blood loss, statistically significant: TAH-150 mL; TLH-50 mL; LSH-50 mL.
  • Length of hospital stay, statistically significant: TAH-75 hrs.; TLH-30 hrs.; LSH-28 hrs.
  • Significantly less estimated blood loss and a significantly shorter length of stay were observed with minimally invasive procedures when compared to total abdominal hysterectomy.
  • After previous use of the Harmonic Scalpel, the authors found this tool to be "unsatisfactory...because of excessive bleeding."
  • The authors report success with the PK Cutting Forceps, finding the PK system to be, "effective for hemostasis, even on large vessels..."
  • The authors prefer advanced bipolar over monopolar energy in proximity to the bowel and bladder because the PK instrumentation appears to cause less thermal injury.
  • For more information regarding LSH, please reference: Bojahr et al. (2009); Cipullo et al. (2009); Einarsson et al. (2010); Erian et al. (2008); Ghomi et al. (2005); Sarmini et al. (2005); and Zupi et al. (2003).
Reference
  • Hoffman C., Kennedy J., Borschel L., Burchette R., Kidd A. Laparoscopic Hysterectomy: The Kaiser Permanente San Diego Experience. J. Minimally Invasive Gynecology. 2005; 1 ; p. 16-24.

Levy

Key Point
  • Article is an overview of vessel-sealing devices for laparoscopic procedures including: PKS Cutting Forceps and Plasma Trissector (Trissector is no longer sold), Harmonic Scalpel, Ligamax 5 Endoscopic Multiple Clip Applier, EnSeal Tissue Sealing and Hemostasis System and EnSeal PTC, and LigaSure V.
  • Dr. Levy's conclusions seem to be based on opinion rather than factual clinical data.
    - No numerical data were presented related to burst pressure.
    - Please reference Lim et al. (1999) for systolic blood pressure and the "Securing Vessels" tab.
  • Article does not favorably portray the Gyrus PKS system.
  • Key Points relating to "Securing Vessels" for the PK System:
    - PK devices, specifically the PKS Cutting Forceps, have been providing adequate hemostasis for over 10 years without a need for a vessel-sealing claim.
    - The PK platform has the largest array of instrument tips for the surgeon's specific needs.
    - There is a 7 mm vessel-sealing claim for the PKS SEAL Open forceps.
    - The PKS Cutting Forceps has serrated jaws to grasp onto tissue whereas tissue tends to slip through the jaws with LigaSure.
    - PK is the only platform that provides surgeons with true bipolar cut and bipolar coagulation in one instrument. LigaSure can coagulate with bipolar but has a monopolar cut with the Advance device.
  • The article references Newcomb et al. (2009), and Lamberton et al. (2008).
Reference
  • Levy, B. Vessel-Sealing Devices. OBG Management. September 2009; 21 (9).

Wang

Key Point
  • Compared the effectiveness of the PKS Cutting Forceps with the Kleppinger bipolar forceps in laparoscopically assisted vaginal hysterectomy (LAVH).
  • Average blood loss: PKS Cutting Forceps-197 mL; Kleppinger-253 mL (not statistically significant).
  • Average OR time: PKS-87.6 minutes; Kleppinger-93.4 minutes (not statistically significant).
  • PKS Cutting Forceps generated less plume and thermal tissue damage compared to the Kleppinger forceps.
  • PKS Cutting Forceps demonstrated less sticking and required fewer instrument changes than the Kleppinger forceps.
Reference
  • Wang C., Yuen L., Yen C., Lee C., Soong Y. Comparison of Efficacy of Pulsed Bipolar System and Conventional Electrosurgery in Laparoscopically Assisted Vaginal Hysterectomy. Laparoendoscopic & Advanced Surgical Techniques. 2005; 15 (4) p. 361-364.
