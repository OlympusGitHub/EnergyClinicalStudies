Bojahr

Key Point
  • Retrospective analysis of the first 1,000 consecutive laparoscopic supracervical hysterectomy (LSH) cases for one gynecologist with the purpose of analyzing the surgeon's learning curve.
  • Cases broken out into 4 groups by uterine weight during years 2002-2006.
  • Mean extirpated uterine weight: 212.5 grams.
  • Median OR time for 1,000 cases: 70.9 min.
  • Duration of surgery depending on extirpated uterine weight:
  • Median OR time was reduced from 85.4 minutes in year one (1) to 72.4 minutes in year four (4), while average uterus weight rose from 192.3 grams to 228.7 grams over the same timeframe.
  • An experienced surgeon can rapidly learn the laparoscopic techniques necessary for LSH with an expected learning curve of about 30 cases before the surgeon gains proficiency of technique.
Reference
  • Bojahr B., Tchartchian G.,Ohlinger R. Laparoscopic Supracervical Hysterectomy: A Retrospective Analysis of 1000 Cases. J. of the Society of Laparoendoscopic Surgeons. 2009; 13; p. 129-134.
