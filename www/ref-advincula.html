Advincula

Key Point
  • Examined the surgical outcomes of myomectomy through the traditional laparotomy (29 patients) and robot assist laparoscopy approaches (29 patients).
  • Although the hospital and professional costs were significantly greater for the robotic approach, the professional reimbursement was not significantly different compared to the reimbursement of the open approach. This shows that in this study the surgeon was not compensated for the increased time the robotic approach required.
    - Total charges for robot assisted laparoscopic myomectomy: $36,030 Hospital $30,084; Professional $5,946.
    - Total reimbursement for robot assisted laparoscopic myomectomy: $15,444 Hospital $13,181; Professional $2,263.
    - Total charges for open myomectomy: $18,065. Hospital $13,401; Professional $4,664.
    - Total reimbursement for open myomectomy: $8,857 Hospital $7,015; Professional $1,842.
  • Difference between total cost vs. total reimbursement: Robot: 20,586; Open: 9,028.
  • Statistically significant difference between robot and open groups. - Average blood loss: Robot: 195.7 mL.; Open: 364.7 mL.
    - Length of stay: Robot: 1.48 days; Open: 3.62 days.
    - OR time: Robot: 263.75 min.; Open: 154.41 min.
  • Due to 5-year depreciation costs model at the study's institution, the robot added an average of $10, 568.94 to every robot case.
Reference
  • Advincula A., Xu X., Goudeau S., Ransom S. Robot-assisted Laparoscopic Myomectomy versus Abdominal Myomectomy: A Comparison of Short-Term Surgical Outcomes and Immediate Costs. J. Minimally Invasive Gynecology. 2007; 14 (6); p. 698-704.
