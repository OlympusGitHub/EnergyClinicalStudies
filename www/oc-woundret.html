Cohen

Key Point
  • Examined four (4) different types of retraction devices for open surgery, Gyrus Protractor, Alexis Wound Retractor, Mobius Abdominal Retractor, Balfour metal retractor.
  • Total exposed surface area:
    - Gyrus Protractor: 93.9 cm.
    - Balfour: 79.3 cm.
    - Alexis: 77.0 cm.
    - Mobius: 61.0 cm.
  • The Gyrus Protractor provides improved exposure. "Its low profile design allows the surgeon to roll the ring tight to maximize the snugness against the incision, achieving the utmost exposure while minimizing hand fatigue and tissue damage during lengthy procedures."
  • The Gyrus Protractor maintains tissue moisture, which can decrease wound desiccation, and offers wound protection.
  • "The Gyrus Protractor has now become this surgeon's retractor of choice."
Reference
  • Cohen, S. & Schleifer W. Perfect Exposure For Open Surgery: A Prospective Trial Comparing Four Retractors. White Paper - 2007.

Pelosi

Key Point
  • Examined the use of the Gyrus Protractor over 72 consecutive cases.
  • Gyrus Protractor system offers a uniform circumferential retraction resulting in the maximum radial exposure for a given incision, regardless of size and thickness of the abdominal wall.
  • The even distribution of force (offered by the Gyrus Protractor) cannot be generated by conventional metal retractors unless an excessive number of retractors are used.
  • The Gyrus Protractor is self-adapting to the thickness of the abdominal wall, making it an ideal tool for obese patients.
  • "In our experience, this new soft, atraumatic, self-retaining retractor is easy to apply, facilitates exposure through a small incision, and improves the efficiency of surgical maneuvers through mini-laparotomy incisions."
Reference
  • Pelosi II MA, Pelosi III MA. Self-Retaining Abdominal Retractor For Minilaparotomy. Obstet Gynecol. 2000 Nov; 96 (5 Pt 1): 775-8.
