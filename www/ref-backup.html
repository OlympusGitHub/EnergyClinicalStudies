Key Point

This shows that in this study the surgeon was not compensated for the increased time the robotic approach required.

    - Total reimbursement for open myomectomy: $8,857 Hospital $7,015; Professional $1,842.

    - Length of stay: Robot: 1.48 days; Open: 3.62 days.

AAGL

Key Point
  • This special article represents the AAGL's (American Association of Gynecologic Laparoscopists) position regarding the laparoscopic and vaginal vs. open approach for hysterectomy.
  • "It is the position of the AAGL that most hysterectomies for benign disease should be performed either vaginally or laparoscopically and that continued efforts should be taken to facilitate these approaches."
  • "When hysterectomy is necessary, the demonstrated safety, efficacy, and cost-effectiveness of vaginal hysterectomy and laparoscopic hysterectomy mandate that they be the procedures of choice."
  • "When hysterectomy is performed without laparotomy, early institutional discharge is feasible and safe, in many cases within the first 24 hours."
  • Laparoscopy reduced the indirect costs of hysterectomy by as much as 50% when compared to abdominal hysterectomy.
  • "A number of clinical situations considered as contraindications to laparoscopic hysterectomy seem not to have merit when subjected to critical analysis". These include obesity and previous caesarian section.
  • "If a large uterus or other coexisting disease or surgical considerations render vaginal hysterectomy not feasible, laparoscopic hysterectomy seems to be a safe alternative that preserves most of the advantages of vaginal hysterectomy over abdominal hysterectomy."
Reference
  • AAGL, AAGL Position Statement: Route of Hysterectomy to Treat Benign Uterine Disease. AAGL Advancing Minimally Invasive Gynecology Worldwide. J. Minimally Invasive Gynecol. 2010 Nov. 3.

Clements

Key Point
  • Examined the bursting pressure of secured arteries and the incidence of failure to adequately secure 4 mm and 5 mm arteries in a porcine model using the SonoSurg and Harmonic ACE instrumentation.
  • Each device divided 44 arteries between 4 and 5 mm.
  • SonoSurg and Harmonic ACE secured 4-5 mm arteries with similar burst pressures, with no statistically significant differences.
  • SonoSurg and Harmonic ACE secured over 90% of tested vessels.
  • "There is no difference in mean burst pressure between the two devices when used to [secure] 4 mm and 5 mm arteries. The incidence of failure to [secure vessels] adequately at initial transaction and failure to achieve a burst pressure of 360 mmHg is the same for each device."
  • Burst pressures tested are much higher than mean systolic blood pressure or blood pressure of hypertensive person during exercise (worst case scenario), which has been documented to max out at approximately 240 mmHg, with a mean value of 189 mmHg (Lim et al. 1999). Mean systolic blood pressure for an average person is 119 mmHg (Lim et al. 1999).
Reference
  • Clements RH, Palepu R. In Vivo Comparison Of The Coagulation Capability Of Sonosurg And Harmonic Ace On 4 mm And 5 mm Arteries. Surg Endosc. 2007 Dec;21(12): 2203-6.

Eisenkop

Key Point
  • Compared laparoscopic outcomes in endometrial cancer to the historical outcomes of the same procedure performed through the "open" approach.
  • Retrospective study examined 200 patients undergoing the laparoscopic (TLH) and 95 patients undergoing the open (TAH) total hysterectomy with bilateral salpingoophorectomy and pelvic/aortic lymph node dissection.
  • Average OR time, statistically significant: TAH - 128.3 min.; TLH - 139.5 min.
    - Hoffman et al. 2005 reported similar mean OR time, TLH-131 min.
  • Average estimated blood loss, statistically significant: TAH - 284.7 mL.; TLH - 155.2 mL.
    - Hoffman et al. 2005 reported similar mean blood loss: TLH-130 mL.
  • Average total lymph node yield, statistically significant: TAH - 25.7; TLH - 34.7.
  • Average length of hospital stay, statistically significant: TAH/BSO/LND - 7.9 days; TLH/BSO/LND - 3.2 days.
  • 5 mm PKS Cutting Forceps, PKS OMNI and PKS Spatula were used in this study.
  • Laparoscopic approach yielded larger nodal yield, reduced blood loss, less transfusions, some reduction in complications, reduced hospital stay, statistically longer but clinically equivalent operative time and some differences in pathology.
  • "Operative outcomes of the current series confirm a report of pulsed bipolar cautery producing hemostasis more effectively than conventional bipolar cautery for laparoscopic radical hysterectomy."

Fanning

Key Point
  • This study can be used with surgeons who may use the robot or be considering the robot for cytoreduction by showing that laparoscopic cytoreduction utilizing the PKS Cutting Forceps is an effective approach for ovarian cancer.
  • 11 patients underwent laparoscopic cytoreduction.
  • Median OR time: 2.5 hr.
  • Median blood loss: 275 mL.
  • Median length of stay: 1 day.
  • Median ovarian size was 5 cm and median omental metastasis was 14 cm.
  • Laparoscopic cytoreduction for the treatment of primary advanced ovarian cancer with 82% of cases successfully debulked laparoscopically without conversion to laparotomy.

Hur

Key Point
  • Examined the incidence of vaginal cuff dehiscence after different modes of hysterectomies (abdominal, vaginal, laparoscopic assisted vaginal and laparoscopic).
  • 7286 patients; 7039 total hysterectomy, 247 supracervical hysterectomy.
  • All TLH procedures used PKS Lyons Dissector to ligate vessels, monopolar Endoshear scissors to make the colpotomy.
  • All LAVH procedures used traditional scalpel, clamps, and sutures.
  • The overall cumulative incidence of vaginal cuff dehiscence in the study was 0.14% (10 cases).
  • The cumulative incidence of vaginal dehiscence from January 2005-March 2006 was higher for TLH where monopolar was used for the colpotomy: TLH-4.93%; TVH-0.29%; TAH-0.12%.
  • Of the 10 total cuff dehiscences reported in this study, TLH has a statistically significant risk of cuff dehiscence incidents.
    - 80% occurred after a TLH procedure; 10% TAH; 10% TVH.
  • Exact cause of the increase in cuff dehiscence after 2005 is unknown but the hypothesis in this study is that the use of monopolar energy on the cuff likely causes more necrosis and devascularization compared to colpotomies dissected abdominally or vaginally (using scalpel and sutures).
  • For more information regarding cuff dehiscence with TLH, please reference Jeung et al. (2010), Kho et al. (2009), and the "da Vinci" tab.
Reference
  • Hur H.C., Guido R.S., Mansuria S.M., Hacker M.R., Sanfilippo J.S., Lee T.T. Incidence and Patient Characteristics of Vaginal Cuff Dehiscence After Different Modes of Hysterectomies. J. Minim Invasive Gynecol. 2007; 14 (3); p. 311-7.
