Key Point
  • This paper has been handed out by your competitors. The purpose of this summary is to give you data to counter the argument that your competitors are making.
  • Examined burst rates of PKS Cutting Forceps, Harmonic, EnSeal, & LigaSure devices in porcine models. Numbers for Trissector not included in the summary below.
  • No statistical difference between burst pressures of CF and competitors for 2-3mm or 6-7mm vessels.
  • Mean burst pressure:
    - Vessel diameter 2-3 mm (mmHg):
    - SurgRX EnSeal-1025.3
    - Gyrus PKS Cutting Forceps-910.3
    - LigaSure V with Force Triad-999.4
    - Ligamax-819.6
    - Harmonic Scalpel-789.9
    - LigaSure V-744.4
    - Vessel diameter 4-5 mm (mmHg):
    - LigaSure V-1261.5
    - Ligamax-940.0
    - SurgRX EnSeal-927.9
    - LigaSure V with Force Triad-884.7
    - Gyrus PKS Cutting Forceps-647.5
    - Harmonic Scalpel-390.8
    - Vessel diameter 6-7 mm (mmHg):
    - Ligamax-756.9
    - SurgRX EnSeal-720.5
    - LigaSure V-645.2
    - Harmonic Scalpel-532.2
    - LigaSure V with Force Triad-386.5

    - Burst pressures tested are much higher than mean systolic blood pressure or blood pressure of hypertensive person during exercise, which has been documented to max out at approximately 240 mmHg, with a mean value of 189 mmHg. Mean systolic blood pressure for an average person is 119 mmHg (Lim et al. 1999).
