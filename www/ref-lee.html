Lee

Key Point
  • When comparing the metrics of blood loss, OR time, and postoperative complications in laparoscopic radical hysterectomy, pulsed bipolar energy (PKS)is more effective than conventional bipolar electrosurgery (Kleppinger).
  • Significantly less blood loss in the pulsed bipolar group (PKS Cutting Forceps):
    - Blood loss: PKS-397 mL.; Kleppinger-565 mL.
  • Significantly less OR time in the pulsed bipolar group (PKS Cutting Forceps):
    - OR time: PKS-172 min.; Kleppinger-229 min.
  • PKS system, "eliminates the risk of electrical injury", allows for "fewer instrument changes", and "does not stick to coagulated tissue and relatively less extensive thermal damage makes meticulous hemostasis possible."
  • "PlasmaKinetic cutting forceps showed a cutting ability equal to that of [conventional bipolar] electrosurgery, less plume generation, and less thermal tissue damage."
  • The PKS Cutting Forceps reduce the workload for the surgeon by allowing "simultaneous performance of hemostasis and division" in one instrument.
  • Paper does not state thermal margin figures.
Reference
  • Lee C.L., Huang K.G., Wang C. J., Lee P.S., Hwang L.L. Laparoscopic Radical Hysterectomy Using Bipolar System: Comparison with Conventional Bipolar Electrosurgery. Gynecologic Oncology. 2007; 105; p. 620-624.
