Hur

Key Point
  • Examined the incidence of vaginal cuff dehiscence after different modes of hysterectomies (abdominal, vaginal, laparoscopic assisted vaginal and laparoscopic).
  • 7286 patients; 7039 total hysterectomy, 247 supracervical hysterectomy.
  • All TLH procedures used PKS Lyons Dissector to ligate vessels, monopolar Endoshear scissors to make the colpotomy.
  • All LAVH procedures used traditional scalpel, clamps, and sutures.
  • The overall cumulative incidence of vaginal cuff dehiscence in the study was 0.14% (10 cases).
  • The cumulative incidence of vaginal dehiscence from January 2005-March 2006 was higher for TLH where monopolar was used for the colpotomy: TLH-4.93%; TVH-0.29%; TAH-0.12%.
  • Of the 10 total cuff dehiscences reported in this study, TLH has a statistically significant risk of cuff dehiscence incidents.
    - 80% occurred after a TLH procedure; 10% TAH; 10% TVH.
  • Exact cause of the increase in cuff dehiscence after 2005 is unknown but the hypothesis in this study is that the use of monopolar energy on the cuff likely causes more necrosis and devascularization compared to colpotomies dissected abdominally or vaginally (using scalpel and sutures).
  • For more information regarding cuff dehiscence with TLH, please reference Jeung et al. (2010), Kho et al. (2009), and the "da Vinci" tab.

Jeung

Key Point
  • Examined the incidence of vaginal cuff dehiscence as a result of a total laparoscopic hysterectomy (TLH).
  • 248 patients; 124 interrupted figure-of-eight suture group, 124 two-layer running suture group.
  • Colpotomy was performed using a monopolar needle over a rotating vaginal blade.
  • There were three (3) cuff dehiscence incidents reported.
  • No statistically significant difference between the average OR time, blood loss, and length of stay metrics.
    - Average OR Time:
    Figure-of-eight: 109.8 minutes.
    Two-layer running: 115.0 minutes.
    - Average blood loss:
    Figure-of-eight: 79.6 minutes.
    Two-layer running: 78.5 minutes.
    - Average length of hospital stay:
    Figure-of-eight: 3.9 days.
    Two-layer running: 3.9 days.
  • No statistically significant difference between two suture closure groups. Authors conclude that further study is needed with a larger group of patients.
  • For more information regarding cuff dehiscence with TLH, please reference Hur et al. (2007), Kho et al. (2009), and the "da Vinci" tab.
  • For more information regarding TLH, please reference: Hoffman et al. (2005)
Reference
  • Jeung I.C., Baek J.M., Park E.K., Lee H.N., Kim C.J., Park T.C., Lee Y.S. A Prospective Comparison of Vaginal Stump Suturing Techniques During Total Laparoscopic Hysterectomy. Arch Gynecol Obstet. 2010; 282 (6); p. 631-8.

Kho

Key Point
  • Examined the incidence and characteristics of patients with vaginal cuff dehiscence after robotic cuff closure in the robotic assisted total laparoscopic hysterectomy. In this paper, monopolar energy is used to close the cuff.
  • 510 patients.
  • Twenty-one (21) of 510 patients were identified with vaginal cuff dehiscence, an incidence of 4.1%.
  • Cuff dehiscence defined as "full thickness separation, partial or total, of the anterior and posterior edges of the vaginal cuff with or without bowel evisceration."
  • "The extent of thermal injury from the electrocoagulation on the vaginal wall and its effects on healing are currently unknown...but thermal effect and vaginal closure technique probably play major roles."
  • For more information regarding cuff dehiscence with TLH, please reference Hur et al. (2007), Jeung et al. (2010), and the "da Vinci" talk tracks located under the "National Sales Meeting 2010" tab of this Binder.
Reference
  • Kho R., Akl M., Cornella J., Magtibay P., Wechter M., Magrina J. Incidence and Characteristics of Patients with Vaginal Cuff Dehiscence After Robotic Procedures. Obstetrics & Gynecology. 2009; 114 (2, part 1); p. 231-235.
